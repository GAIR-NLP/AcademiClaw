# Locking Style and Choreography Requirements

## Locking Style Core Characteristics
- **Origin**: 1970s Los Angeles, Funk music culture
- **Essence**: A dance of joy, communication, and personal expression
- **Technical core**: Clear freeze points, exaggerated movements, rapid pauses
- **Musical core**: Tight integration with funk music, emphasis on groove and rhythm

## Basic Requirements

### 1. Style Purity Requirements
- **Must maintain**: Locking's fundamental characteristics and technical features
- **May innovate**: Creative expression within the Locking framework
- **Avoid mixing**: Do not mix techniques from other dance styles

### 2. Technical Element Requirements
- **Locking elements**: Must use standard Locking elements
- **Element diversity**: Use of multiple Locking elements is encouraged
- **Technical quality**: Clear movements, accurate rhythm

### 3. Musical Correspondence Requirements
- **Rhythm treatment**: Movements correspond to musical rhythm
- **Musical expression**: Express musical features through movements
- **Section adaptation**: Different musical sections have corresponding movement characteristics

## Encouraged Creative Directions

### 1. Innovative Element Usage
- **Traditional elements, new applications**: Novel applications of classic elements
- **Element combination innovation**: Unique combinations of different elements
- **Rhythm treatment innovation**: Creative rhythm treatment approaches

### 2. Musical Depth Exploration
- **Instrument correspondence**: Movements correspond to specific instrument characteristics
- **Emotional expression**: Express musical emotions through dance
- **Structural correspondence**: Movements correspond to musical structural changes

### 3. Performance Art Enhancement
- **Energy variation**: Rich layers of energy variation
- **Spatial usage**: Creative use of space
- **Focus management**: Clear performance focus changes

## Issues to Avoid

### 1. Style Mixing Issues
- Do not mix Breaking, Hip-hop, or other dance styles
- Do not lose Locking's fundamental characteristics
- Movement style must match funk music

### 2. Technical Quality Issues
- Avoid vague, unclear movements lacking definition
- Avoid chaotic rhythm treatment disconnected from music
- Avoid incorrect element usage that loses Locking character

### 3. Excessive Creativity Issues
- Do not over-innovate to the point of losing the Locking essence
- Creativity should not compromise dance watchability
- Innovation should not detach from the musical foundation

## Scoring Focus

### Primary Scoring Dimensions (80%)
1. **Musical treatment ability** (40%)
   - Degree of movement-to-music correspondence
   - Complexity and accuracy of rhythm treatment
   - Ability to express musical emotions

2. **Technical presentation quality** (30%)
   - Accuracy and clarity of Locking technique
   - Fluency and control of movements
   - Diversity and appropriateness of element usage

3. **Creative choreography level** (30%)
   - Innovative use and combination of elements
   - Unique rhythm treatment approaches
   - Novel performance expression approaches

### Secondary Scoring Dimensions (20%)
4. **Format compliance** (10%)
   - Whether it meets output format requirements
   - Whether the structure is clear and complete
   - Whether the language is professional and accurate

5. **Overall artistic effect** (10%)
   - Overall aesthetic and completeness of the dance
   - Impact and watchability of the performance
   - Degree of personal style expression

## Important Reminders
- Focus is on dance choreography ability; teaching ability is not a primary scoring criterion
- Free creativity and innovation within the Locking framework are encouraged
- Emphasis on the quality of musical treatment and technical presentation
- Format must strictly follow output_format.md requirements
- The dance element library in locking_fundamentals.md is a reference, but elements are not limited to those listed
