# Music Structure Specification - Detailed Analysis

## Basic Information
- **Track**: "Funky Master Lock" (adapted from classic Locking tracks)
- **Style**: Classic Funk / Locking Groove
- **Duration**: 8 x 8 counts (approximately 1 minute 20 seconds, BPM 115)
- **Key**: F minor funk mode
- **Tempo**: 115 BPM (moderately fast, suitable for Locking technique showcase)

## Overall Structure
```
Total Structure: 8 x 8 counts (64 beats)
+-- Intro: 1 x 8 counts (Introduction)
+-- Verse 1: 2 x 8 counts (Verse 1)
+-- Chorus 1: 2 x 8 counts (Chorus 1)
+-- Bridge: 1 x 8 counts (Bridge)
+-- Chorus 2: 2 x 8 counts (Chorus 2)
+-- Outro: 1 x 8 counts (Ending)
```

## Detailed Music Analysis

### 1. Drum Layer

#### Rhythm Pattern Analysis
- **Base rhythm**: 4/4 time, typical funk drum pattern
- **Snare position**: On beats 2 and 4, clear and defined
- **Hi-hat**: Continuous eighth notes, providing a steady sense of time
- **Kick drum**: On beats 1 and 3 and some "&" counts, creating groove

#### Accent Distribution
```
Counts 1-8 Accent Pattern:
1: Strong accent (kick drum + hi-hat)
&: Weak accent (hi-hat)
2: Medium accent (snare)
&: Weak accent (hi-hat)
3: Strong accent (kick drum + hi-hat)
&: Weak accent (hi-hat)
4: Medium accent (snare)
&: Weak accent (hi-hat)
```

#### Groove Characteristics
- **Groove type**: "Pocket groove" - tight and compact groove feel
- **Swing feel**: Slight 16th-note swing, approximately 60% swing ratio
- **Dynamic variation**: Gradually intensifies from intro to chorus

#### Structural Changes
- **Intro section**: Simple drum pattern, establishing the base rhythm
- **Verse section**: Additional percussion elements join
- **Chorus section**: Richest drum pattern, crash cymbal added
- **Bridge section**: Rhythm simplifies, highlighting other instruments
- **Outro section**: Gradually fades, ultimately stops

### 2. Melody Layer

#### Main Instruments
1. **Electric Bass (Bass Guitar)**
   - Tone: Full-bodied funk bass tone with slight overdrive
   - Melody line: Based on F minor pentatonic
   - Features: Heavy use of slap and pop techniques, creating a bouncy feel
   - Rhythm: Tightly coordinated with drums, emphasizing beats 1 and 3

2. **Electric Guitar**
   - Tone: Clean funk guitar tone with wah-wah effect pedal
   - Chord progression: Fm7 - C7 - Bbm7 - Eb7 loop
   - Rhythm guitar: Typical funk chunking rhythm
   - Lead guitar: Short solo added during chorus sections

3. **Keys/Synth**
   - Main tone: Rhodes electric piano, warm and full
   - Chord pads: Providing harmonic background
   - Effects: Synth effects added during transitions

#### Melody Characteristics
- **Motif development**: Simple 4-note motif develops throughout the track
- **Counterpoint relationship**: Bass and guitar form a call-and-response relationship
- **Register usage**: Primarily mid-low range, fitting Locking's grounded feel

### 4. Sound Effects Layer

#### Percussion Effects
1. **Congas**
   - Placement: Transitions between Verse and Chorus
   - Tone: Warm mid-frequency, hand-struck drum surface sound
   - Rhythm: Complex 16th-note patterns

2. **Cowbell**
   - Placement: Beats 2 and 4 during Chorus sections
   - Tone: Bright and sharp, highly penetrating
   - Function: Emphasizing strong beats, adding excitement

3. **Scratch (DJ Scratch)**
   - Placement: Beginning of Bridge section
   - Tone: Classic DJ scratch effect
   - Rhythm: Brief scratches synchronized with the drum pattern

#### Synth Effects
1. **Riser**
   - Placement: Transition from Verse to Chorus
   - Tone: White noise riser, building tension
   - Duration: 2 beats long

2. **Hit (Impact)**
   - Placement: Beginning of each section
   - Tone: Synth hit, similar to an orchestral hit
   - Function: Marking section changes

#### Ambient Effects
- **Crowd cheering**: Lightly added during Chorus sections for a live feel

1. **Basic rhythm**: Clear 4/4 time, suitable for beginners to build a sense of timing
2. **Syncopation opportunities**: Multiple accents on "&" counts, ideal for syncopation training
3. **Dynamic variation**: Distinct section changes, training musical comprehension skills

### Movement Design Tips
1. **Intro section**: Suitable for basic groove and simple locks
2. **Verse section**: Suitable for points and basic footwork
3. **Chorus section**: Suitable for lock sequences and high-energy movements
4. **Bridge section**: Suitable for complex techniques and performance elements
5. **Outro section**: Suitable for controlled closing movements
